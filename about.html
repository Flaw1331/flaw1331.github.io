My Story


Youth

My journey began in Kansas City - I was born in one of the suburbs (Overland Park) and have since decided to stay a bit. I attended Blue Valley High School and graduated in the spring of 2009. During my youth, I spent most of my time outside playing sports or riding bikes or jumping on the trampoline. I excelled in athletics and was on track to continue my career in college until an injury forced me to consider other options.

College

I chose to attend the University of Kansas and study at the School of Engineering after I graduate from high school. I, initially, majored in Computer Engineering but soon found that my passion starting to wane and my focus lacking. I pledged Triangle Fraternity my first semester and never looked back. I made many lifelong friends through the Brotherhood that still persist to this day. After doing some serious soul-searching, I decided it was best to stop spending money on school if I wasn't going to take it seriously and instead, find a way to make money to support myself.

The Dark Days

After leaving school, I didnt feel like I had much direction in my life. I spent a good portion of my time continuing to soul-search and find "my path". I was working odd hours (overnight shifts) at Sprint and it was starting to add up and take its tole on me. I made a few moves within the company and was able to position myself in such a way that led to advancement. Although this helped keep me going initially, I still needed to make a change.

Present Day

Throughout my adult life, the running joke about my hobbies has always been "why don't you make a spreadsheet for that". If I was playing the newest Madden, of FIFA, or whatever video game - I always found a way to consume and analyze all the data the game had to offer. Finally, someone sent me some information on a new program involving Big Data Analytics. I thought it sounded perfect for what I wanted to do! I applied immediately and was accepted - I started my prep work ASAP. After countless hours working and honing my skills, I graduated! Now I seek my next great challenge in life...
